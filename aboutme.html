About Me

My Backstory

I was born and raised in rural Kentucky, and have spent most of my life on a farm in the small town of Bardstown, Kentucky. In highschool, I taught myself how to code, and discovered game development through small coding projects.

I fell in love with game design, and quickly got passionate about it. Once I got to college, I was able to begin taking classes in game design and animation, and expanded my skillset out by joining the CMU Game Creation Society (GCS). Throughout my year and a half at CMU, I have worked on more than a dozen game projects, and I hope to work on many more before I leave!

Education

I am currently a sophomore at Carnegie Mellon University, studying Computer Science, Game Design, and Animation. Before coming to CMU, I was fortunate enough to be able to take summer courses in Game Design at the University of Washington, Biophysics and Biofluid Dynamics at Standford, and Astronomy and Environmental Science at Yale.
